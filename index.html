About

I am a PhD candidate in Earth & Environment at Boston University. My work focuses on detecting land-use and land-cover change from satellite imagery, quantifying the carbon implications of utility-scale solar development in temperate forests, and building reproducible quantitative workflows for environmental decision-making.

I am especially interested in how remote sensing, time-series analysis, and carbon modeling can be combined to support the clean energy transition while minimizing land-use impacts.

Projects

Mapping Utility-Scale Solar and Forest Loss in Massachusetts (2005–2024)
Remote sensing · Google Earth Engine · Object-based change detection
  • Detected utility-scale solar farms and associated deforestation using CCDC time-series modeling and SNIC segmentation on Landsat imagery.
  • Distinguished between solar built with and without forest clearing, and non-solar land-cover change.
  • Implemented the full workflow in Google Earth Engine with post-processing and accuracy assessment in R.
Carbon Emissions from Forest Conversion for Solar Energy
Carbon bookkeeping · Monte Carlo · R
  • Developed a bookkeeping model to estimate CO₂ emissions from forest clearing associated with utility-scale solar projects.
  • Incorporated biomass variability, decay pathways, and uncertainty from both area estimates and carbon stocks.
  • Implemented Monte Carlo simulations in R to provide confidence intervals for annual and cumulative emissions.
Remote Sensing Change Detection Toolkit
Reproducible workflows · GEE · R/Python
  • Building a small collection of reusable scripts for time-series change detection, object-based classification, and accuracy assessment.
  • Designed to support rapid prototyping of land-use change analyses for energy and land-use planning questions.
